The Factoring Calculator

This is a factoring calculator developed by David Sai. Put in a quadratic equation, for example: 6x^2 + 8x + 2

and it will return the factored form (2x+1)(3x+2) and (x+1)(6x+2). The reason I decided to make a factoring calculator

is that when I was learning how to factor quadratics, I constantly found that there were not many available.

How to use:

Before you use this, I highly recommend learning how to factor polynomials first. It is always better to know how to do it yourself

Follow the instructions that are there.

How to factor polynomials

First, you should look at the first term (ax^2). If "a" is 1, then follow the steps below.

  1. Look at the second and third terms, and take the coefficients of both of them
  2. Figure out what two numbers multiply to the third coefficient (c) and add to the second (b). This will take a fair amount of trial and error.
  3. Take those two numbers and put them into (x + _)(x + _)

If the first coefficient is not 1, follow these steps.

  1. Multiply the first coefficient and the third coefficient
  2. Figure out which two numbers multiply to this product and add to the second coefficient. Let's call these numbers m and n.
  3. Put all the variables you know in a grid pattern like the one below.
  4. We don't know the variables U, W, Y, or Z yet, but we can figure that out
  5. Think of it as a multiplication table. Fill in U, W, Y, and Z.
  6. Your answer will be (w + u)(y + z)
